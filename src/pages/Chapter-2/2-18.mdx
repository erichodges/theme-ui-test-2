# prakāśa-kriyā-sthiti-śīlaṃ bhūta-indriya-ātmakaṃ bhoga-apavarga-arthaṃ dṛśyam

#### प्रकाशक्रियास्थितिशीलं भूतेन्द्रियात्मकं भोगापवर्गार्थं दृश्यम्

#### The seen has the qualities of brightness, activity and stay, its nature is elements and senses, taking the role of our essence its purpose is worldly enjoyment and emancipation

<br />

**prakāśa** - brightness <br />

**kriyā** - activity; bodily activity; medical treatment or practice <br />

**sthiti** - staying or remaining or being in any state or condition; maintenance of life; <br />

**śīla** - nature <br />

**bhūta** - element; living being <br />

**indriya** - faculty of sense; sense; organ of sense <br />

**ātmaka** - taking the role of our essence <br />

**bhogā** - worldly experience <br />

**apavarga** - emancipation <br />

**artha** - purpose, aim; meaning; thing, object <br />

**dṛśya** - the seen <br />

<br />

#### Commentary by TKV Desikachar:

The world exists to set us free.

#### Commentary by Paul Harvey:

Yoga is not about not enjoying the world because we see it as it really is.
Rather it is seeing the world as it really is and still enjoying it.
