### [<](/Chapter-2/2-19/) 2-20 &nbsp[>](/Chapter-2/2-21/)

# draṣṭā dṛśi-mātraḥ śuddhaḥ api-pratyaya-anupaśyaḥ

#### द्रष्टा दृशिमात्रः शुद्धोऽपि प्रत्ययानुपश्यः

#### The seer alone sees, although pure it observes through psychic activity.

<br />

**draṣṭṛ** - seer <br />

**dṛśi** - seeing; the power of seeing <br />

**mātrā** - only; measure, quantity, sum, size, duration <br />

**śuddha** - pure <br />

**api** - even, also, very; something more; moreover <br />

**pratyaya** - psychic activity; cause; conception, assumption, notion, idea; <br />

**anupaśya** - apperceive; one who sees; perceiving; seeing; observes <br />

<br />

#### Commentary by Paul Harvey:

“What we observe is changing,
What we observe with is changing,
Where we observe from is unchanging.”
