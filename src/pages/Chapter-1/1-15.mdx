### 1-15

# dṛṣṭa-anu-śravika-viṣaya-vitṛṣṇasya vaśīkāra-saṃjñā-vairãgyam

#### दृष्टानुश्रविकविषयवितृष्णस्य वशीकारसंज्णावैराग्यम्

#### Absence of thirst towards objects, either as seen or following heard spiritual teachings, is the acknowledgement of the mastery of dispassion

<br />

**dṛṣṭa** - seen <br />

**anu** - follow <br />

**śravika** - heard spiritual teachings <br />

**viṣaya** - object <br />

**vitṛṣṇasya** - absence of thirst <br />

**vaśīkāra** - mastery <br />

**saṃjñā** - acknowledgment <br />

**vairāgya** - dispassion <br />

<br />

#### Commentary by T Krishnamacharya:

Detachment can imply an attachment elsewhere.

<br />

#### Commentary by Paul Harvey:

Absence of thirst towards objects,
whether material or spiritual,
is the acknowledgement of the mastery of dispassion.
