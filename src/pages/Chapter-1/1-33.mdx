### [<](/Chapter-1/1-32) 1-33 &nbsp[>](/Chapter-1/1-34/)

# maitrī-karuṇā-muditā-upekṣānāṃ sukha-duḥkha-puṇya-apuṇya-viṣayāṇāṃ bhāvanātaḥ citta-prasādanam

#### मैत्रीकरुणामुदितोपेक्षाणां सुखदुःखपुण्यापुण्यविषयाणां भावनातः चित्तप्रसादनम्

#### The psyche can be calmed by cultivating as an object friendliness, compassion, gladness and disinterest within happiness, suffering, virtue and vice

<br />

**maitrī** - friendliness, good will <br />

**karuṇā** - compassion, pity <br />

**muditā** - gladness; rejoicing in, delighted; joyful <br />

**upekṣā** - disinterest, overlooking, disregard, indifference <br />

**sukha** - pleasure; happiness; agreeable; ease; comfortable; pleasant <br />

**duḥkha** - suffering, pain, sorrowful <br />

**puṇya** - propitious; virtuous; meritorious <br />

**apuṇya** - non-meritorious; wicked; unvirtuous <br />

**viṣaya** - object <br />

**bhāvana** - cultivation; imagining, fancying; forming in the mind; to occupy one's imagination with <br />

**citta** - psyche (the totality of the human mind, conscious and unconscious); mind; heart <br />

**prasādana** - calming <br />

<br />

#### Commentary by T Krishnamacharya:

“Bhāvana is a beneficial attitude
that is consciously cultivated
despite tendencies to the contrary.”

<br />

#### Commentary by Paul Harvey:

Maitrī
“Cultivating a feeling of friendliness
towards our own attempts,
let alone other’s demands,
to distract ourselves.”
