# tataḥ pratyak-cetanā-adhigamaḥ-api-antarāya-abhāvaḥ ca

#### ततः प्रत्यक्चेतनाधिगमोऽप्यन्तरायाभवश्च

### From that turning inwards consciousness is attained and also the non-appearance of the interventions

<br />

**tatas** - hence, from that <br />

**pratyak** - turning inwards <br />

**cetanā** - consciousness <br />

**adhigama** - attained <br />

**api** - even, also, very; something more; moreover <br />

**antarāya** - intervention, obstacle <br />

**abhāva** - non-appearance, non-existence, absence <br />

**ca** - and <br />

<br />

#### Commentary by T Krishnamacharya:

“It is through Praṇavo Japam
that the true nature of the Jīva is realised.”

<br />

#### Commentary by TKV Desikachar:

“The more you try to know Īśvara,
the more you come to know your self.”

“If you don’t know yourself how can you think of something which is more than you or higher than you?”

<br />

#### Commentary by Paul Harvey:

“From that turning inwards,
awareness is attained
and the non-appearance of the interventions.”
